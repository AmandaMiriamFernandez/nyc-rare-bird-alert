Grading the Groundhogs: Who's Actually Good at Predicting the Weather?

Punxsutawney Phil may be the most famous, but NOAA data shows he's one of the worst forecasters in the bunch.

By Amanda Fernandez

Every February 2nd, millions of Americans tune in to watch Punxsutawney Phil emerge from his burrow. But when NOAA ranked 19 weather-predicting groundhogs based on their accuracy over the past 20 years, Phil didn't exactly shine.

Using March temperature averages for the U.S., NOAA graded each groundhog's predictions to see who's actually worth listening to. The results? The most famous groundhog is nearly the least accurate.

The Full Rankings

The bars show each forecaster's accuracy rate -green is good, yellow is middling, and red means you'd be better off flipping a coin.

What about Phil?

Punxsutawney Phil, the nation's most celebrated groundhog, ranks 17th out of 19 with a 35% accuracy rate. That means he gets it wrong about two out of every three years. Even taxidermied groundhogs like Poor Richard (50%) and Uni the Groundhog (47.37%) have better accuracy than Phil.
